Here’s a fun, lesser-known Roman tidbit:

Gladiators were often organized by weight class, experience, and fighting style—kind of like modern boxing divisions—so referees could intervene if a match got too one-sided, and the crowd (or the emperor) would decide whether a defeated but still-living gladiator should live or die.
